Core curriculum

11 modules covering the actual job

Not generic admin training — the specific workflows private practice runs on. Each module includes a lesson, flashcards for revision, and a quiz to mark it complete.

01

Diary & clinic-list management

Structuring a consultant's diary across multiple sites, managing clinic lists, and handling the inevitable last-minute changes without losing the day.

02

Dictation-to-letter workflow

Turning consultant dictation into accurate, properly formatted clinic letters — efficiently, and to the standard private practice expects.

03

Records & referrals

Managing patient records and the referral process end to end, including the handoffs between GPs, consultants, and other specialists.

04

Billing & insurer admin

Private billing, self-pay administration, and insurer invoicing — the financial side of the job that's rarely taught properly anywhere.

05

Multi-consultant coordination

Supporting more than one consultant at once without anything falling through the cracks — prioritisation, communication, and the systems to make it sustainable.

06

Investigations, imaging & results

Managing diagnostic requests, imaging pathways, pathology results, report tracking and consultant review — safely, from request through to follow-up.

07

Theatre, procedures & hospital coordination

Coordinating procedure bookings, theatre lists, pre-assessment and admissions across consultants, hospitals and insurers.

08

Consultant private practice setup & management

How a private practice actually runs — systems, workflows, insurer relationships, financial admin and compliance.

09

Systems, software & digital skills

Patient management systems, electronic records, insurer portals, spreadsheets and the cyber security basics every secretary needs.

10

Patient communication & customer service

Professional phone and email standards, managing expectations, confidentiality, and handling complaints in private healthcare.

11

Advanced skills & career development

Moving from reactive admin into proactive, senior support — prioritisation, leadership, service improvement and a path toward practice management.
