How to Price Your Day Rate as a Private Medical Secretary

Pricing your own work is one of the harder parts of going self-employed — and it's easy to under-price out of caution, especially early on. Here's the core formula most UK freelancers use, and what actually moves your number up or down.

The basic formula

(Target annual income + business expenses + a buffer for gaps) ÷ billable days per year = your day rate

Broken down:

  1. Start with your target income — what you actually want to take home this year, before tax.
  2. Add your expenses — insurance, equipment, software, training, accountancy fees.
  3. Add a buffer for gaps — most freelancers plan around 220–230 working days a year, not 260+, to account for holiday, illness, and quiet periods.
  4. Divide by billable days, not calendar days.

The result is a starting point, not a ceiling — your specialty experience, local demand, and how much notice you need all shift it from there.

What actually moves your rate

  • Specialty experience — deeper experience in a specific area is worth pricing for, not giving away.
  • System proficiency — genuine experience with the practice management systems a consultant already uses removes onboarding friction for them, which is worth something.
  • Urgency and notice — same-day or short-notice cover reasonably commands more than a booking made weeks out.
  • Minimum booking length — hourly bookings price differently than half- or full-day minimums.
